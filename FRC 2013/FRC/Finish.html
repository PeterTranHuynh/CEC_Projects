Finish VI Template Appearance

When first opening the Finish VI and enter it's block diagram, it should appear to look something like the picture to the left. The Finish VI is the file in charge of deactiviating the robot and all of it's related devices. As seen in the picture, the way the robot deactivated is by closing all motors, devices, references, and save data and then stopping all loops inside other VIs. The way this is done is by simply having a two frame flat sequence structure. The first frame handles all deactivations, and the second stops all loops.

Extending Finish.VI

Add any motor, relay, camera, joystick, or other devices connected to robot into the first frame. As seen in the screenshot, our team added all of our devices into the flat sequence. Notice that we originally intended to use relays for specific motors, as compared to standard motors, there is only a slight different in LabVIEW programming when call relays, just use the appropriate objects found inside the WPI library and you'll do fine.
